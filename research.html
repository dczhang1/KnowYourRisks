Research Overview

Our research investigates the complex dynamics of workplace risk-taking behavior, examining how individual, organizational, and environmental factors interact to influence decision-making in professional settings.

Personality & Preferences

Individual differences in risk tolerance and decision-making styles across different domains

Organizational Context

Company culture, policies, and leadership approaches that shape risk-taking behavior

Situational Factors

Environmental conditions and specific circumstances that influence risk decisions

Risk Taking at Work

A comprehensive model of workplace risk behavior

Our Research Approach

Through a multi-method approach, we examine how these factors interact to influence risk-taking behavior in professional settings:

Quantitative Surveys

Large-scale data collection across industries

Behavioral Experiments

Controlled studies of decision-making

Field Observations

Real-world analysis of risk behaviors

Current Projects

Workplace Risky Choice Inventory Development

Bottom-up approach to generating and analyzing risky choices at work, developing a comprehensive 69-item inventory

Calculated and Spontaneous Risk-Taking Scale (CASPRT)

Development and validation of a dual-process approach to understanding risk-taking styles across seven studies

Risk Preference and Well-Being Meta-Analysis

Examining the relationship between risk preference and subjective and psychological well-being

Future Directions

Our upcoming research initiatives focus on advancing our understanding of workplace risk-taking behavior through innovative methodological approaches and theoretical explorations.

Construct Validity of Risk Propensity

Conducting validation studies on the "general risk factor" and exploring construct validity of specific risk-taking factors

Measurement Methodologies

Investigating differences between "revealed" and "stated" measures of risk preference, exploring their psychological constructs

Cognitive Processes in Risk Taking

Exploring the relationship between risk preference and cognitive abilities, examining psychological mechanisms underlying risky choices

Positive vs Negative Risk Taking

Developing theoretical frameworks to distinguish between constructive and destructive risk-taking behaviors in organizational contexts
